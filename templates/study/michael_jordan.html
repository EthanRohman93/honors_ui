Michael Jordan

About Michael Jordan

Michael Jeffrey Jordan, also known by his initials MJ, is a former professional basketball player and businessman. He is often considered the greatest basketball player of all time. Jordan played 15 seasons in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Chicago Bulls and Washington Wizards. His biography on the official NBA website states, "By acclamation, Michael Jordan is the greatest basketball player of all time."

Career Highlights

  • 6× NBA champion (1991–1993, 1996–1998)
  • 5× NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP)
  • 14× NBA All-Star
  • 10× scoring champion (1987–1993, 1996–1998)
  • 1988 NBA Defensive Player of the Year

Early Life

Born on February 17, 1963, in Brooklyn, New York, Michael Jordan was raised in Wilmington, North Carolina. Showing an early talent for sports, Jordan was a standout at Laney High School and later played college basketball at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he helped his team win the 1982 NCAA Championship with a game-winning shot.

Post-Retirement

After his final retirement in 2003, Jordan became the majority owner of the Charlotte Hornets, an NBA team. He is also involved in various business ventures and philanthropic efforts, contributing to the community through the James R. Jordan Foundation.
